Game Modes

Brawl stars is known for its many unique game modes and concepts of team modes as well as a battle royale. There are currently nine main game modes but there have been many more temporary ones.

Solo Showdown

Solo showdown is a single player game mode in which 10 players spawn at different areas of the map with no unfair advantage where ever you spawn. The goal is to collect power cubes which provide extra health and damage and stacks to efficiently eliminate the other 9 brawlers. You collect trophies based on your final position meaning if you come first you gain a lot whereas if you come last you lose a lot.

Duo Showdown

Duo Showdown is another type battle royale game mode where teams of 2 will be placed against 4 other teams of 2. In this game mode your main goal is also collecting cubes whilst eliminating other enemies. If you or your teammate die at any point they will have the chance to revive which is done so by waiting for them to respawn. However if both you and your teammate are dead then the game is over for you. You collect trophies based on your ranking meaning if you are to come first you will gain a lot whereas if you place last then you will lose a lot.

Brawl Ball

This game mode is currently the most popular game mode in all of brawl stars and it requires 2 teams of 3 players. The main goal is to score 2 goals to win the game. However both teams are allowed to try eliminate each other to provide an advantage and clearing out the opponents to score a goal. If you are currently holding/dribbling the ball you cannot attack enemies. If you manage to score 2 goals or are in a 1-0 lead by the end of overtime you have won the game and will gain trophies. If it is the end of overtime and you both have scored either 0 or 1 goals then it is a tie where no one receives trophies. If the opponent wins then you will lose trophies.

Bounty

Bounty is another 3v3 game mode where the objective is to kill the enemy as many times as possible. If you successfully kill an enemy you will receive an extra star on top of your head which is your “bounty”. The more stars you have the bigger target you are because if the enemy manages to kill you or your teammate who has a big bounty they will collect that for their team and increase their points. Whoever has the most points at the end of the game wins. If there is a tie in the score the winner is decided by whichever team currently owns the blue star.

Duels

Duels is a 1v1 game mode where you another person face off using a team of 3 different brawlers. The goal is to eliminate the opponents 3 brawlers before they eliminate your team.

Gem grab

Gem grab is a 3v3 game mode and is the first game mode everyone is introduced to when starting the game. The objective is to collect 10 gems which spawn in the middle which triggers a countdown and when it reaches 0 you win the game. If a brawler dies while carrying gems, the gems will drop and anyone is free to take them. If both teams are able to get 10 gems a countdown will not start until one team gets another gem. If there is one team with 10 gems and the countdown starts, the countdown may be disrupted by either killing a brawler who has gems or collecting enough gems in the middle to tie the gem count.

Heist

Heist is another 3v3 game mode in which the primary objective is to go to the opponents side and destroy the safe. The safe’s health will depend on the average power level of all 6 brawlers in that game. There is a possibility of a tie whether it’s killing each others safe at the exact same time or if both safe’s have the exact same percentage at the end. Hot zone

Hot zone is another 3v3 game mode which requires brawlers to stand in a certain area in order to gain percentage points. Once a team reaches 100% the game is over. Different maps have different amounts of hot zones ranging from 1-3.

Knockout

Knockout is a 3v3 game mode which players have to eliminate each other before the enemy team. Once you die you will not respawn until the next round. A round finishes when one team has successfully eliminated all 3 enemy brawlers. The game is finishes when a team manages to win 2 rounds. There is a possibility of a tie and if the final score remains 0-0 or 1-1, both teams will not receive or lose any trophies.

Wipeout

Wipeout is a 3v3 game mode similar to knockout however you will respawn once you die and the objective is to reach 10 kills before the enemies. A tie is possible if at the end of the timer both have the same score or reach 10 kills at the exact same time.

Special Game Modes

Special game modes come more rarely than the normal ones and they rotate meaning you will only get the same special game mode every once in a while. These game modes currently include boss fight, big game and robo rumble.

Boss Fight

This game mode requires you and 2 other teammates to take down a massive robot which has many special abilities. These abilities not only increase every time you pass a level, but the boss also may go into different modes. These modes get harder and harder to deal with so it is best killing it before it gets too hard.

Big Game

This mode requires 6 players where 5 players are trying to take down 1 player. The player on their own will get a massive health, damage and speed buff. This game mode can be compared to as a juggernaut game mode. The goal for the 5 players is to take down the single player as quickly as possible whilst the single player has to remain alive until the timer ends. There is no other way for anyone to win.

Robo Rumble

This special game mode requires a team of 3 to take on a robot invasion. The players goal is to protect their safe which is placed in the middle of the game. The more levels you pass the more difficult it becomes to survive and protect. In every level there are 9 waves. If you die at any point you will respawn but there is a time delay.
